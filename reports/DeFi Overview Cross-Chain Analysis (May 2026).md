DeFi TVL has contracted to $85.6B, down 42% from the November 2025 peak, but the ecosystem's revenue capacity remains strong.

Daily fees of $55M roughly match the 2021 highs despite half the locked capital, indicating a structural improvement in how efficiently DeFi monetizes liquidity.

The chain economy is becoming more diversified. While Ethereum still anchors 55% of TVL, execution-heavy chains such as Solana and Polygon turn over capital at 3–6x Ethereum's rate**.** Hyperliquid has emerged as the third-largest fee earner ($364M YTD).

With CLARITY poised to accelerate TradFi integration with DeFi infrastructure, the focus shifts from current headline TVL to each chain’s ability to capture distinct products, participants, and use cases.

This article breaks down where capital is productive, where fees are concentrated, and how chain-level DeFi economies differ.

**TLDR**;

- DeFi TVL at $85.6B, down 42% from Nov 2025 peak.
- Daily DeFi fees of $55M match 2021 levels at only half the capital, doubling TVL efficiency and Dex velocity.
- The cross-chain DeFi landscape is diversifying. Ethereum anchors 55% of TVL, but Solana leads Dex volume by 33%, and Hyperliquid is #3 in fee generation from derivatives.
- Dex, lending, perps, prediction markets, launchpads, risk curators, and liquid staking all make meaningful shares across chains and contribute to a durable DeFi landscape.

DeFi TVL stands at $85.6B, down 42% from the $147B November 2025 peak and roughly 48% below the 2021 high, partly reflecting the lower asset prices against the USD.

Despite the decline in locked capital, daily fees of $55M roughly match the Q4 2021 peak of $ 50M. This suggests that fees earned per dollar locked have structurally improved over four years.

Dex volume is running at approximately $6.6B/day, with the 30d MA in the [$5-7B](https://x.com/search?q=%245-7B&src=cashtag_click) range, down from [$10B+](https://x.com/search?q=%2410B%2B&src=cashtag_click) at the start of 2025. Solana leads with 30% market share, followed by Ethereum and Binance.

Daily active DeFi addresses remain elevated at 12.5M. These data suggest that while liquidity has contracted, user activity and protocol monetization remain resilient.

## TVL Efficiency and Dex Velocity

TVL Efficiency and Dex Velocity proxies how productively capital is being used within the ecosystem.

TVL Efficiency (fees/TVL) has roughly doubled since 2021: currently 0.062% vs 0.03% in Q4 2021. DeFi protocols are earning 2x more fees per dollar of liquidity in comparison.

This signals structural improvements in the DeFi landscape: more mature fee tiers such as concentrated liquidity and dynamic fees, broader diversification of DeFi primitives, and the growth of higher-margin product categories. TVL efficiency has improved +15.9% YTD, even as TVL and volume declined.

Dex Velocity (vol/TVL) sits at 0.077, within the normal 0.06-0.10 range. Despite the TVL drawdown, the remaining capital is being actively traded. Note that the current Dex volume only includes spot and prediction markets, not perps. Given that perp protocols generate $26B volume daily, the composite DeFi velocity is likely higher.

## Chain Breakdown

Nine chains currently hold TVL above $ 1B. Ethereum ($44.2B) dominates, but its share has drifted from 60% to 52% over the past 12 months.

A competitive mid-tier has formed: Solana ($6.0B), BSC ($5.6B), Bitcoin ($5.2B), Tron ($5.1B), and Base ($5.0B) are all clustered in the [$5-6B](https://x.com/search?q=%245-6B&src=cashtag_click) range. Arbitrum ($1.59B), Hyperliquid ($1.53B), and Polygon ($1.18B) follow.

Volume dominance tells a different story. [@solana](https://x.com/@solana) makes 32% of DEX volume despite holding only 7% of TVL. Ethereum's share declined from 70% in 2021 to ~19% since 2025. Base has gained a share at 12%.

Fee dominance is similar, but [@HyperliquidFndn](https://x.com/@HyperliquidFndn) stands out as the third with $364M in earnings YTD (9% of total DeFi fee share) and $4M daily. Ethereum generates $10M (39%) daily, next to [@solana](https://x.com/@solana) at $6M (30%).

A divergence between liquidity depth and capital velocity is a notable feature of the current DeFi landscape. Execution-heavy chains such as [@solana](https://x.com/@solana) (0.15 velocity), [@base](https://x.com/@base) (0.17), and [@SuiNetwork](https://x.com/@SuiNetwork) (0.14) turn over capital at roughly 3-6x the rate of [@ethereum](https://x.com/@ethereum) (0.03). These ecosystems support more frequent trading and active strategies.

By contrast, lower-velocity chains like Ethereum and Tron hold capital for activities such as yield, collateral, and custody.

## Fee and TVL Compositions

TVL composition reveals how each chain's DeFi capital is allocated. Ethereum's TVL is diversified across Liquid Staking (23%), Lending (17%), Staking Pool (12%), and a growing RWA segment (11%). Solana follows a similar staking-led profile but with more capital in Dex liquidity pools. Base is concentrated in Lending (48%) and Polygon in prediction markets (33.5%).

Fee composition varies sharply across chains. Ethereum's fees are spread across Liquid Staking (33%), Lending (21%), and CDPs (16%). Solana's $4.9M skews toward Dexs (39%), Staking (33%), and Launchpads (23%). Hyperliquid generates half of its fees from derivatives, with another 39% from its staking pools. [@0xPolygon](https://x.com/@0xPolygon) is almost entirely Prediction Market (95%), driven by [@Polymarket](https://x.com/@Polymarket).

A broadening mix of use cases now contributes meaningfully to fee generation. Diversified product streams suggest the DeFi ecosystem is maturing, building durable economics rather than cycling on liquidity alone.
